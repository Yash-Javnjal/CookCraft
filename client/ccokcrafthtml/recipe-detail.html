Rustic Heirloom Tomato Galette

"Don't skip salting the tomatoes first!"

The Gatherings

  • 1 1/4 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup unsalted butter, cold and cubed
  • 1 1/2 lbs mixed heirloom tomatoes, thick sliced
  • 1/2 cup whole milk ricotta
    *Drain excess moisture
  • 1 egg beaten (for wash)
  • Fresh thyme and flaky sea salt
format_quote

"The secret ingredient is curiosity."

Method

01

Prepare the Dough

In a large bowl, whisk the flour and a pinch of salt. Cut in the cold butter until the mixture resembles coarse meal. Drizzle ice water, 1 tablespoon at a time, until dough just holds together. Shape into a disc, wrap tightly, and chill for at least 1 hour.

02

Salt the Tomatoes

Arrange tomato slices on a paper towel-lined baking sheet. Sprinkle generously with salt and let sit for 20 minutes to draw out excess moisture. Gently pat dry before assembling to ensure a crisp crust.

03

Assemble & Bake

Preheat oven to 400°F. Roll dough into a 12-inch circle. Spread ricotta leaving a 2-inch border. Shingle tomatoes over the cheese. Fold edges over, brush with egg wash, and bake for 35-40 minutes until golden. Garnish with fresh thyme.
